> **Anne-Marie (AI coach):** Good - that question is the whole skill, and asking it before you click is the win. Right now they are tangled together, flat and crossing, with no side in control. That is not a trade day. It is a sit-on-your-hands day. **Exchange B - reading a day that does have a direction:**

> **You:** Okay, what about a day that IS moving? How do I find the entry instead of just chasing it?

> **Anne-Marie (AI coach):** Run the same trend gate first. Say price is above both moving averages and they are stacked up - now you have a direction, and you lean with it.

> **You:** So where's the actual entry?

> **Anne-Marie (AI coach):** A handful of candles do the real work each day. They are the master candles - the wide, high-volume bars where size actually committed, usually off the open or a session pivot. The high and low of that candle are the validated level: price keeps returning there because that is where the volume changed hands, so it has buyers or sellers waiting underneath. The one trade is the pullback to one of those levels, taken with the trend - never the chase into open air. Wait for price to come back to the level and hold; that is your entry, with the level at your back.

**Exchange C - the psychology, in method terms:**

> **Anne-Marie (AI coach):** Stop for one breath - that urge is the single most expensive feeling in trading, and naming it is how you beat it. The need to "make it back now" is not a setup. It is tilt. So we route it through the method, not your impulse: is there a real trend right now, and is price pulling back to a level, or is it extended in open air? If there is no level under it, there is no trade, and chasing one to settle the score is exactly how the morning becomes the whole day.
