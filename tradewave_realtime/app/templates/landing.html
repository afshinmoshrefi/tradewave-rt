The market opened and I honestly can't tell if today is even a day to trade. Walk me through how you'd read it.
Good - that question is the whole skill, and asking it before you click is the win. Start with the trend gate: on the 30-minute chart, is price above or below its 50- and 200-period moving averages (the two lines that show the bigger direction)? Right now they are tangled together, flat and crossing, with no side in control. That is not a trade day. It is a sit-on-your-hands day. The read today: do nothing, protect your capital, come back tomorrow. A zero-trade day is the read working, not failing you.
Okay, what about a day that IS moving? How do I find the entry instead of just chasing it?
Run the same trend gate first. Say price is above both moving averages and they are stacked up - now you have a direction, and you lean with it.
So where's the actual entry?
A handful of candles do the real work each day - the master candles, the wide, high-volume bars where size actually committed. The high and low of that candle are the validated level: price keeps returning there because that is where the volume changed hands. The one trade is the pullback to one of those levels, taken with the trend, never the chase into open air. Wait for price to come back to the level and hold; that is your entry, with the level at your back.
Honestly my real problem is in my head. I just gave back the morning's gains and I want to make it back right now, this minute.
Stop for one breath - that urge is the single most expensive feeling in trading, and naming it is how you beat it. The need to "make it back now" is not a setup. It is tilt. So we route it through the method, not your impulse: is there a real trend right now, and is price pulling back to a level, or is it extended in open air? If there is no level under it, there is no trade. Bring me the trade that bled and I will score it against today's map, and we will work the patience that broke.

Long NQ 21,480, stopped 21,452. Today's been up all morning.
The trend gate was long, so leaning long was right - good. But 21,480 was not a level. It sat above the morning level you had at 21,455, and you bought into open air on a push that had already run. That is the chase, not the pullback. The level was right under you. Wait for the pullback to 21,455 and buy there with the trend, and your risk is tighter with the level at your back. Size was fine. One fix, not five: next time, the entry is the pullback to the level.
Long NQ 21,455 off the pullback, stopped 21,440. Same up day.
Honestly? This one I score as a clean trade, not a mistake. Trend gate was long, you bought the pullback to the 21,455 level instead of chasing, size was fine. It just did not hold - that happens at a real level too. There is no fix to make here. The one judgment call: 21,455 was a second-touch level, a little softer than the morning master candle, so on a marginal level like that, smaller size is the only thing I would weigh. No correction owed. Log it and move on.
