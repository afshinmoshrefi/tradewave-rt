# TradeWave Realtime - First-Month UX Study (absolute beginner to expert)

> Generated 2026-06-16 by a 16-agent simulation: 6 realistic personas living all 31 days of the launch month (Jun 24-Jul 24, incl. 8 weekends + the Jul 3 holiday) against the REAL coach behavior, 8 adversarial dimension critics, and 2 synthesists (~2M tokens). Companion to `ARCHITECTURE.md` and `LAUNCH_RISKS.md`.

## Executive summary

The honest state of the TradeWave Realtime coach is: a genuinely warm front door bolted onto a product that only fully serves one narrow user - an already-competent intraday trader who shows up every weekday morning, trades the levels, and chats heavily. For that person the daily Level Map saves real time, the same-day briefing feels like insider access, and (after enough engagement) the memory and continuity compound. For everyone else, the product quietly fails the founder's "never leave them lost" bar, and it fails in a consistent, structural way rather than through one-off bugs.

The through-line of failure is this: every feature that would catch a struggling, drifting, or beginner user is gated behind behavior that exact user does not produce, OR is unbuilt while being promised, OR derives session-state from one broken clock function. Concretely, four root systems break the bar across all six personas. (1) Onboarding routes by psychological struggle, not skill, with only a binary beginner-vs-default posture and NO Stage 0 foundation - so a never-traded retiree (Maya) is assigned "the four master candles," a 7-year vet (Sam) gets sizing-101, and "new to futures" (Devin) dodges beginner detection entirely. There is a cliff at both ends and a one-shot assignment frozen for a month. (2) The clock is wrong on exactly the low-traffic days people have the most free time: is_session_day is weekday-only with no holiday table, so on Fri Jul 3 the entire product confidently tells trusting users the market is OPEN - the single most trust-destroying moment in five of six journeys; a Friday 4-5pm off-by-one tells users the market reopens "tomorrow" (a Saturday); and the check-in card asks "What's your plan today?" on Saturdays and the holiday while the same page says markets are closed. (3) Memory is engagement-gated and inverted: the rolling summary needs 8 messages and the flagship "Last time you told me" greeting needs a review check-in - so the lightest, most at-risk users (Priya, Maya) get a coach that on Day 30 knows exactly what it knew on Day 2, while a spiraling user (Tom) is cheerfully greeted with a quote from his best day. (4) The product is structurally blind to trades and to drift: the promised debrief is unbuilt (yet sold on the landing page), the coach only knows a trade if typed, never proactively asks, and there is zero re-engagement wiring (no last_active field, only the welcome email) - so a discouraged user who blows an eval or revenge-trades and goes dark is met with total silence right through the renewal window, and at the Day-30 "am I getting better?" moment the coach can offer only prose, never proof.

The net retention picture: the two competent daily-trader personas (Renee, Sam) and the fragile-but-resolved returning trader (Tom) renew, but cooled, qualified, and one bad month from churn - kept by the map, the price lock, and emotional bond rather than by the product solving their problem. The busy low-engager (Priya) probably renews for the levels feed alone, with no relationship to lose. The true beginner (Maya) and the prop-rookie (Devin) are the clearest casualties - Maya probably pauses/churns and Devin survives only on his own sunk-cost. The coach is excellent at on-demand, plain-language Q&A and warm at intake; it loses people on follow-through, calendar truth, proof of progress, and ever reaching back when they slip.

## Experience by level

### Absolute beginner / true zero (Maya, 58, retired teacher, never traded)
- **Day 1:** Best emotional moment of her month. The 6-question intake in Anne-Marie's voice lands the 'someone is paying attention to me' feeling exactly as designed; beginner posture correctly fires on 'brand new, never traded' and the warm wrap-up reflects her back. But the same Day 1 quietly seeds the failure: with no behavioral struggle keyword, resolve_assignment falls to DEFAULT_ASSIGNMENT and hands a woman who has never seen a chart 'Grade the trend first' and 'the four master candles.' The Today page is a wall of level numbers and templates ('stair-step, 30-min 50/200 SMA stack, institutional candle') that mean nothing to her, and the check-in card asks 'What's your plan today?' when she has no plan.
- **First week:** A diligent split-screen: the on-demand coach is genuinely excellent when she asks plainly ('what is a candle?', 'what is ES?', 'what is a moving average?') - this is the single feature she values - but she has to bail OUT of the structured Learn path into chat to get the foundation, and nothing she learns in chat is markable, so her on-ramp lesson count stays at 0. She hand-assembles her own trading-101 one question at a time. Her light engagement (3-4 messages over 3 days) keeps the rolling summary empty, so the coach is not yet compounding.
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** The dead zone where she has the MOST time and gets the LEAST. The Today page honestly labels the stale weekend map, but the check-in card asks 'What's your plan today?' on Saturday - a contradiction she reads as the site being broken. Worst: on the Jul 3 holiday the product confidently tells a brand-new user the market is OPEN (is_session_day blindness) - she is the user least equipped to know it is wrong, and if she tried to 'follow along live' she would sit before a frozen market thinking she broke something. The long Jul 3-5 weekend is three days of confusion-then-silence with no re-engagement.
- **Month end:** At the exact renewal-decision moment she asks 'how am I doing overall, am I making progress?' and the product cannot answer. Same two focus lessons as Day 1; no completed beginner path, no graded sim trades (debrief unbuilt), no skill milestone, no reply to the beginner-path question she deferred to Anne-Marie on Day 16 (deferral dead-ends with no notification). The coach is kind but can show her nothing concrete the month produced.
- **Verdict:** PROBABLE CHURN (likely pauses rather than hard-cancels; ~60% gone by Day 60). She churns not because the coach is bad but because the product never solved HER problem: no absolute-beginner foundation, no daily reason to return for a non-trader, dead weekends/holiday made worse by bugs, engagement-gated memory that never fires for a light user, a deferral black hole, and zero re-engagement during her slow taper. The pause-don't-cancel billing flow is the only thing likely to retain her - a billing feature, not earned value.
- **Biggest risk:** The curriculum has no floor under her: she is the most diligent, most emotionally invested user the product will ever get, and on Day 1 it assigns her content she cannot read and gives her no Stage 0 to complete - so she repeatedly feels stupid (the exact failure mode she signed up to escape) and reaches Day 30 with nothing to point to as proof the month mattered.

### Beginner-in-instrument / prop rookie (Devin, 31, ~1yr stocks, just funded a Topstep futures eval)
- **Day 1:** Hot start - delighted by the personal intake and the daily levels, which give him concrete numbers for his first futures morning, and the coach nails his 'what is a tick / MES vs ES' questions from the Reference corpus. But his phrasing 'new to futures' contains no BEGINNER_HINT, so he is silently filed as a default (non-beginner) member: no term-definition steering, no paper/sim encouragement, despite being a near-absolute beginner in futures. His 'chasing' struggle assigns Stage 5 and Stage 3 lessons that presuppose Stage 1-2 foundations he lacks.
- **First week:** Real value from the map and morning briefings (the sticky retention spine), and good method answers - but the coach never knows about his actual trades (he chases, doesn't type it, doesn't check in), so the 'coach that knows how I'm doing' he wanted never materializes. His one free Saturday to study dead-ends on Stage 1 lesson 1 (the curriculum cliff), and he abandons the Learn tab by Day 8 - a third of the offering goes dead for the rest of the month.
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** Weekends dead-air him (stale map, the buggy Saturday 'plan today?' card, no content). The Jul 3 holiday is the credibility hit: he asks 'is it a holiday?' and the coach, told it always knows the session state, confidently says the market is open. The Friday 4-5pm bug also tells him the market reopens 'tomorrow' on a Friday. Three low/no-value days across the long weekend, with his last interaction being a wrong answer.
- **Month end:** He blows his Topstep eval mid-month (Day 14) on a revenge-chase, hits the coach tilted, gets warm-but-generic psychology with no knowledge of the actual trade and no follow-up, then drifts for 10 days met with total silence. He resurfaces on his own sunk-cost, stumbles into review check-ins on Day 27 (26 days late), and only then gets the continuity feeling he was sold on Day 1. Day 30: a solid-but-thin levels-plus-method product that never did the thing it sold him.
- **Verdict:** AT RISK, leans KEEP for one more month on the thinnest thread (~50-55% 60-day survival). He renews on the locked $99 rate + the sticky levels habit + an up-note final week - NOT because the coach won him. The relationship (a coach who knows and grows him) was the differentiator he paid for, and it stayed latent all month.
- **Biggest risk:** The product cannot see the gap between his real futures level and its only two postures, so it never steers a prop rookie to sim, never teaches the operational reality of a funded account (order entry, eval rules), and never debriefs the trade that blows his eval - the exact moment a coach should have caught him.

### Intermediate / core target (Renee, 44, ~3yr funded, psychology problem)
- **Day 1:** Strongest possible first impression for the core customer: the reflect-back nails her revenge/red-day pattern in Anne-Marie's voice, the two assigned psychology lessons are the right topic, and the map is genuinely useful to a real trader. But her actual failure mode - blowing her daily LOSS LIMIT - is silently missed: bare 'blow' doesn't match the sizing pattern (which needs 'blew'/'blow up'), so she is never assigned a risk/loss-limit lesson and the single most actionable lever for her is skipped.
- **First week:** The core use case works: an in-the-moment, post-loss talk-down (Day 2) actually stops her revenge-trading - the thing that justifies the subscription. The review check-in becomes her post-close confession booth, and by Day 6 she crosses the 8-message gate so the summary starts compounding. But she exhausts both assigned lessons by Day 6 with no deeper psychology/risk-limit track to graduate into.
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** The Friday 4-5pm bug (Day 3) is the first time the coach says something she KNOWS is wrong ('Globex reopens tonight' on a Friday) - corrosive for an experienced trader. Saturday's 'plan today?' card is jarring. The Jul 3 holiday-blindness lands two false-calendar days back-to-back over the long weekend, the most fragile stretch of her month, with zero weekend value and no Sunday-prep nudge.
- **Month end:** On her worst day (Day 8) she goes dark during the spiral and the system does nothing because it can't see her trades or her loss count and has no nudge. At Day 29 she asks where the promised debrief is and gets 'it's coming.' At Day 30 she wants PROOF she improved and the coach can offer only prose, not metrics - no streak, no 'walked away X times vs revenge-traded Y times.'
- **Verdict:** YES, she renews - but cooled from 'this could fix me' to 'a useful habit tool that forgets my trades and can't reach me when I spiral.' Kept by the talk-down working twice, the daily map, and the compounded summary. A B-/C+ retain: loyal for now, mentally keeping a list, and a competitor that actually watched her trades and reached out on bad days would take her.
- **Biggest risk:** The coach is purely reactive: her entire reason for subscribing is changing at-tilt behavior, but a coach that only helps when she chooses to open it mid-meltdown helps least exactly when she needs it most - and the loss-limit tool she most needs was never assigned and (by guardrail) can't be set concretely even when she digs to it herself.

### Returning trader with a blow-up scar (Tom, 36, blew $50k, rebuilding, high shame)
- **Day 1:** Intake is the best moment of his month - being asked 'how has it actually been going, no judgment' and naming his fear/hesitation/oversize pattern makes him feel genuinely seen. But the assignment silently drops the revenge/'make it back' lesson - the exact psychology that blew his account - because resolve_assignment uses only the first two struggle keys and the 3-cap fills on sizing+hesitation. The system also has no 'returning trader' posture; he's treated as a generic default.
- **First week:** A sawtooth: good engaged early days (plan check-ins validating 'watch only', a tiny first win), then a Day-6 red impulse trade he's too ashamed to log, and a multi-day disappearance. The product is blindest exactly when he breaks his rule, because catching it depends on him volunteering a review check-in that shame prevents.
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** The Jul 3 holiday: he opens the app specifically to verify the holiday and the coach confidently tells him the market is open - a direct hit on the trust the empathy worked to build. The Saturday 'plan today?' card contradicts the coach which DOES know it's the weekend. The long Jul 3-5 weekend is his highest churn-risk window and nothing bridges it.
- **Month end:** His two shame-driven lapses get zero re-engagement; he returns only on willpower. On Day 23 the continuity greeting cheerfully echoes his best day ('green week, feel good') while he's confessing a relapse - the memory is real but lagging and rose-tinted. His Day-30 'am I getting better?' reflection is genuinely moving but built on a self-curated, rosy record (he hid both bad days) with no trade truth.
- **Verdict:** YES, narrowly - for emotional/identity reasons, not compounding value. Held by the intake and the Day-30 reflection (the two best moments) and the check-in loop giving real-time permission to trade small. A knife's-edge retain: one more uncaught spiral with no outreach, or one more month with no structured path, and he's gone. The product wins him on empathy and loses him on follow-through.
- **Biggest risk:** The product is silent at the precise moment intervention matters - right after he breaks his size rule and hides - and the one personalization it offered (struggle-to-lesson) missed the account-killing revenge pattern he literally typed; both of his in-month spirals were on exactly that pattern, uncaught and unconnected across days.

### Skeptical veteran (Sam, 49, 7-year mostly-green futures vet, came on a friend's word)
- **Day 1:** The map earns his respect - mechanical, fast, saves him 15-20 min of pre-open charting - and his hard test question ('NQ when globex is inside the institutional candle') gets a correct, in-voice answer, so the engine reads as legit. But a 7-year vet is handed 'Risk and position sizing' + 'Get risk small' (because 'oversizing' matched 'siz') with no way to test out, and he mildly bristles: 'I don't need a position-sizing 101.'
- **First week:** He settles into levels-only utility usage fast; the coach keeps padding answers with method he taught himself years ago and has no advanced gear to shift into. He goes looking for the promised trade-grading on Day 3 and hits air (debrief unbuilt). Because he checks in mornings (plan) and never does a review, the 'last time you told me' line never appears and his summary updates only sporadically.
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** The Jul 3 holiday is the single most damaging day of his month: a vet instantly clocks the system insisting the market is open on July 3rd ('if it doesn't know it's a holiday, what else is it getting wrong?'), denting trust in the one thing he relied on - the map. The Friday 4-5pm bug is a second time-awareness error he catches, compounding the 'it doesn't really know the calendar' impression.
- **Month end:** Two genuinely personal coaching moments (Day 8 and especially Day 27, when it names his cocky-oversizing as a PATTERN) earn a sliver of loyalty - but it still can't grade the actual trade or show him the receipts. At Day 30 he asks point-blank whether there's a level of this product for someone who knows her method cold; the honest answer is no advanced track exists. The product makes no closing argument tailored to a vet.
- **Verdict:** RETAINS, soft and at-risk (~50-60% 60-day), for reasons that have little to do with the coach: the map's daily time-save, the $99 founding-rate lock, and 2-3 coaching moments. He is paying for a levels feed with an occasionally-good coach attached. If the map has a bad week or a competitor offers comparable levels plus real trade grading, he's gone.
- **Biggest risk:** There is structurally no second gear for an advanced trader - only-beginner-vs-default postures, struggle-based (not skill-based) assignment, no refinement track, no test-out - so the skeptic he is hits the intermediate ceiling in two weeks, and the one feature that would lock him (grading his real trades against her map) is unbuilt while promised.

### Busy low-engager (Priya, 39, PM, ~2yr, trades 2-3 mornings/wk, ~20 min, barely chats)
- **Day 1:** Real immediate value: the intake lands the 'someone is paying attention' feeling and the ES map is perfect for a 20-minute morning trader; her one chat question gets a good in-voice answer. But her actual trigger ('forces setups when bored') is compressed to the generic 'overtrading' bucket, and because she'll never mark a lesson done, the beginner on-ramp card will show to a 2-year trader forever.
- **First week:** She is the stress test for whether the engagement-gated value works for someone who doesn't feed it - and it doesn't. The map + briefing is a clean 3-minute daily read (the core perishable value working), but she never checks in and rarely sends 8+ messages, so the coaching_summary stays empty and the 'last time you told me' line never appears. On Day 7 she confesses her exact pattern and it leaves no durable trace (below the 8-message gate).
- **Weekends & the Jul 3 holiday:** Saturday's 'plan today?' card is visibly wrong to a detail-oriented PM. The Jul 3 holiday: the coach insists it's a normal open trading day on a marquee holiday a real trader instantly knows is closed - the most credibility-destroying day. The Friday 4-5pm bug can hit any Friday evening, and her Day-17 revenge-loss debrief lands in exactly that window.
- **Month end:** On Day 20 she explicitly tests 'remind me what I keep getting wrong' and the coach can only parrot her Day-1 intake answer - it has learned nothing in 19 days. She revenge-loses on Day 17 (the same pattern as Day 7, treated as new), disappears, and gets no day-after follow-up. At Day 30 'am I getting better?' is answered like a horoscope: warm, on-voice, content-free about HER, because nothing was tracked.
- **Verdict:** PROBABLY RENEWS for the LEVELS, not the coach - a real churn risk by month 2-3. The perishable map + briefing is worth ~$99 to a time-poor PM, which gets the first renewal. But everything sold above a levels feed delivered almost nothing because it's all engagement-gated and she feeds none of it. The first quiet map week or busy stretch with no nudge to pull her back, she cancels with little to miss.
- **Biggest risk:** She is living proof the coach layer fails its own thesis for the at-risk cohort: every compounding mechanism is gated behind engagement she doesn't produce, so the 'coach that knows you' is, for her, a coach that knows only what she typed once - and there's no proactive nudge to recover her after a lapse or a painful loss, the two moments a single email would have saved her.

## Master gap list (prioritized)

### [CRITICAL] G1 - No market-holiday awareness: the product confidently asserts the market is OPEN on a closed holiday
*Dimension:* Time/session-state correctness (the 'never lost in time' promise) · *Effort:* small · *Who it hits:* both - all six personas on Jul 3; recurs every market holiday

is_session_day is weekday-only, so on Fri Jul 3 (Independence Day observed) the Today time_note, the check-in card, the coach's now_context NOW block, and latest_session all treat a closed market as a normal open trading day. Because the coach is explicitly instructed it always knows the session state and must never ask, when a user asks 'is it a holiday?' it confidently says the session is open - worse than silence, and the user least able to know it's wrong (Maya) trusts it. Experienced personas (Sam, Renee, Devin, Priya) instantly clock it as broken: 'if it doesn't know it's a holiday, what else is wrong?'

- **Root cause:** marketdata.py:45-46 `def is_session_day(d): return d.weekday() < 5  # holidays handled by admin override later` - the override was deferred and never built. There is no holiday table anywhere, and three independent surfaces (time_note in main.py, now_context in levels.py, checkin_phase in mentor.py) each re-derive session state from this one weekday-only primitive, making the error total and self-consistent.
- **Fix:** Add a US equity/futures holiday set (CME/NYSE full-holiday + early-close dates for 2026-2027) and make is_session_day consult it; add is_early_close for half-days. Route ALL session-state decisions through this one function so time_note, now_context, latest_session/prior_session, and checkin_phase can never disagree. Give now_context an explicit 'Markets are closed today for {holiday}; next session {date}' branch. As a launch-week stopgap, ship the admin closed-dates override and hard-code Jul 3 2026 before launch (it lands inside launch month).

### [CRITICAL] G2 - Zero proactive re-engagement: a drifting or discouraged user is met with total silence through renewal
*Dimension:* Reason to come back / lifecycle outreach · *Effort:* medium · *Who it hits:* chatbot-side failure that loses the user - every persona's lapses; acute for Devin (10-day post-blown-eval drift), Tom (two shame-disappearances), Priya (post-loss + post-long-weekend), Maya (pre-renewal taper)

Beyond the transactional welcome email, the entire proactive surface is scaffolded but unbuilt. notify.py declares COACH_KINDS (morning_brief, checkin_nudge, reengagement, quiz_invite, milestone) and a complete policy engine, but the only implemented/called sender is send_welcome (auth.py signup). There is no reengagement/nudge sender, no scheduler/timer to fire one, and - confirmed - no last_active/last_login field on User, so the system cannot even DETECT inactivity. A user who drifts from daily to twice-weekly to gone gets nothing, including across the pre-renewal window and the long Jul 3-5 weekend.

- **Root cause:** The proactive layer (kinds, _allowed caps, NotificationLog ledger, _frame shell) was scaffolded but the senders and their scheduled triggers were never built, and the data model tracks no last-activity, so there is no signal to fire on and no job to fire it. Additionally _allowed() gates on weekday-only is_session_day, so even once built it would mis-fire on holidays until G1 is fixed.
- **Fix:** Add last_active_at to User (touch on /app and /coach loads). Build three senders against the existing _frame/_allowed scaffold: send_reengagement (fires after N trading days with no login), send_checkin_nudge / a Sunday-evening 'your week's map is building tonight' touch, and a post-loss/post-lapse follow-up triggered when a review check-in or chat described a hard day. Add one daily systemd timer + Flask CLI command (e.g. flask lifecycle-tick). Reuse the built send_email/_allowed/_frame machinery - this is wiring, not new infrastructure.

### [CRITICAL] G3 - No trade awareness: the promised debrief is unbuilt, the coach never asks, and 'am I getting better?' is unanswerable
*Dimension:* Whether the coach knows/asks about the user's trades · *Effort:* large · *Who it hits:* both - every persona at the Day-30 decision; acute at high-stakes moments (Renee Day 8 blown limit, Devin Day 14 blown eval, Tom Days 6/20 oversize spirals, Priya Day 17 revenge loss, Maya Day 13 first sim trade)

There is no Trade entity in the system (confirmed: no class Trade/Debrief in models.py). The promised debrief - coach receives completed trades and grades them against the day's map - is unbuilt, yet the landing page actively sells it ('Coming for founding members ... sends your completed trades to your coach ... was the entry at a map level, did you size right', landing.html:134,169-170). The coach only knows a trade if the user types it, never proactively asks (except the one after-close review card many never reach), can't grade it, and can't connect a repeated pattern across days. So worst trading moments get generic psychology, good trades go uncelebrated, and at renewal the coach can offer only prose, never a measured progress story.

- **Root cause:** The debrief was scoped as a committed fast-follow but never built; the data model has no trade record, grader, or capture route, and the customer-facing landing page promises it now - manufacturing an expectation the product cannot meet from Day 1.
- **Fix:** Two parts. (1) Honesty now: change landing.html from a present-tense promise to a clearly-labeled roadmap item ('On the roadmap for founding members') - a one-line pre-launch edit that removes the broken-promise churn argument. (2) Ship a minimal Trade model (instrument, side, entry, exit, size-bucket NOT dollar amount, session_date) with a one-tap 'log a trade' surface (manual paste before the NinjaTrader feed exists) and a deterministic grade-against-DayMap pass (was entry within X ticks of a map level, with the trend gate). Persist it so the coach can proactively ask 'how did the setup go?', surface 'this is the 2nd time you forced a bored entry,' and power a Day-30 'your month' progress view.

### [CRITICAL] G4 - No absolute-beginner foundation and a skill-blind, one-shot curriculum that cliffs at both ends
*Dimension:* Onboarding & curriculum fit / progression · *Effort:* large · *Who it hits:* user - Maya (true zero), Devin (futures rookie), Tom (returning), and inversely Sam/Renee/Priya (over-qualified for assigned lessons)

There is no Stage 0 'trading 101' anywhere in the 47 lessons; Stage 1 lesson 1 is 'the four master candles,' which presupposes you know what a candle, session, tick, futures contract, and limit order are. Assignment is by psychological struggle keyword (not skill), so a never-traded beginner with no struggle gets DEFAULT_ASSIGNMENT = ['Grade the trend first','master candles'], a 7-year vet with 'oversizing' gets sizing-101, and there is no advanced/refinement track and no test-out. Assignment is one-shot at intake (re-run only on manual profile edit), so 'current focus' is frozen for a month and the greeting loops the Day-1 lessons. Beyond Stage 1 it cliffs straight into gamma walls / volume profile with nothing in between. Diligent beginners hand-assemble their own basics in chat (unmarkable, so on-ramp stays at 0).

- **Root cause:** The curriculum was authored from an experienced trader's method with no learner floor; personalization keys on struggle at a single point in time with a binary beginner-vs-default posture and no skill model, no progression engine, and no test-out. is_beginner changes chat TONE, not the assigned lessons.
- **Fix:** Add a Stage 0 'Foundations' track (5-8 short markable lessons: candle/chart/timeframe, futures contract/tick/dollar-value, MES vs ES, RTH vs Globex, limit vs market order, how to open a sim account and place a practice trade). In resolve_assignment, branch on is_beginner to assign Stage 0 first. Add depth tiers + a 'test out / I already know this' to clear lessons and unlock the next tier (serves vets). Re-derive focus lessons as lessons are completed (advance to the next stage) and refresh the greeting's focus so it stops looping Day-1 lessons.

### [HIGH] G5 - Engagement-gated memory inverts 'it remembers me' for exactly the at-risk users
*Dimension:* Does it get more valuable over time / does it remember me · *Effort:* medium · *Who it hits:* both - Priya (empty summary all 31 days), Maya (line never fires), Devin (until Day 27), Sam (all month), Tom (Day 13 no echo; Day 23 stale-positive echo while spiraling)

Two compounding mechanisms both require behavior the at-risk cohort doesn't produce. The rolling coaching_summary only regenerates after 8 new messages (SUMMARY_EVERY=8), so a light chatter's summary stays empty and member_context on Day 20/30 is byte-for-byte the Day-2 intake facts. The flagship 'Last time you told me' greeting line only appears after a REVIEW check-in (kind=='review'), so morning-loggers and shame-avoidant users never trigger it; and because it grabs the most recent review by recency-of-review (not recency-of-event), it can cheerfully echo a user's best day while they are mid-relapse. The lightest, most churn-prone users get the least continuity, the opposite of the design intent.

- **Root cause:** Both features were tuned for the heavy engager: the 8-message Haiku threshold has no time/session fallback and folds in only ChatMessage rows (not check-ins); coach_greeting (mentor.py:235-238) filters CheckIn by kind=='review' only, takes the single most-recent row with no staleness/lapse guard, and has no plan-check-in or chat-turn fallback.
- **Fix:** Trigger a summary update on a time cadence too (e.g. also regenerate if >=2 messages AND >2 days since last summary, and seed it from the intake summary immediately at intake_done) and fold check-in text into the summarizer input. Let the continuity line draw from EITHER a review OR a plan check-in OR the last substantive chat turn, with a recency/lapse guard (soften or skip a stale-positive quote; add 'good to have you back' for a returning lapsed user via the new last_active field).

### [HIGH] G6 - Deferral dead-end: 'flagged for Anne-Marie' is a one-way door with no return path
*Dimension:* The 'never lost' guarantee / closing the loop · *Effort:* small · *Who it hits:* user - Maya (Days 16/17/24, beginner-path question; trust breaks), Renee (Day 16, funded-account sizing), Tom (Day 23, vulnerable low-moment), Devin (Day 22, CL)

When the coach emits the exact sentence 'I've flagged this for Anne-Marie's next teaching session,' the question routes to her review queue, but the member is NEVER notified if/when it's answered. When she handles it, the code only sets reviewed=True (admin.py:191, admin.py:293) and publishes a global corpus entry - it never contacts the specific asker. The persona prompt explicitly promises 'her answer comes back into me ... the loop is working,' a promise the code cannot keep. The one time a user reaches past the AI for the human - often at a fragile moment - the loop goes dark, and re-asking just gets re-deferred.

- **Root cause:** The correction loop was designed corpus-first (improve the coach for everyone) with no member-facing return path. The flagged ChatMessage stores the asker's user (thread.user_id) so they ARE identifiable, but no code links a handled review or published answer back to a notification, and no 'answer_ready' notification kind exists.
- **Fix:** On teach_publish/review_handled, look up the flagged message's user and send a templated 'Anne-Marie answered your question' notification (reuse the milestone/quiz_invite kind once G2's sender machinery is live) deep-linking into the coach with the original question pre-loaded. Optionally add a lightweight DeferredQuestion record so the coach can proactively say 'still in her queue' on the user's next visit. ## Forward design - the month that should exist

### Redesigned daily loop

Every member, every calendar day (trading day or not), reliably gets four things in a fixed order so the Today page is never empty and the coach always has a thread to pull. (1) A SESSION-STATE BANNER that is always correct - "Markets open in 2h10m, here is today's map" / "Markets closed for the weekend, Globex reopens Sunday 6pm" / "Markets closed for Independence Day, next session Monday" - driven by ONE holiday-aware is_session_day() so the Today time_note, the check-in card, and the coach now_context can never disagree. (2) A DAILY NEXT-STEP CARD that is always personalized and always actionable, computed deterministically, never blank: on a trading day for a trader it is "Today's map + your one focus" (the map plus the single most relevant lesson or the open trade to debrief); on a non-trading day or for a non-trader it is "Today's one lesson" drawn from their live path (next unfinished foundation/method/refinement lesson) plus a one-tap mark-done; it ALWAYS resolves to a concrete verb (read this, mark this, debrief that, try this in sim). (3) A CONTINUITY GREETING that reflects real, recent state - it draws from the most recent meaningful signal (review check-in OR plan check-in OR a notable chat turn OR a logged trade), is recency- and lapse-aware ("Good to have you back, it has been four days" instead of cheerfully replaying a stale good day), and cites the member's CURRENT focus (which now advances), not the frozen Day-1 pair. (4) A CLOSE-THE-DAY PROMPT in the coach itself, not just a buried card: after the close on a real session day, if the member chatted or has an open logged trade, the coach proactively offers "want to walk through how today went?" - turning the review/debrief from a card the user must find into a coach behavior. The loop's spine: the perishable map is the trader's pull, but the daily next-step + the streak/progress meter is the LEARNER'S pull, so a non-trader has a fresh, concrete reason to return on a Saturday and a holiday, not just an inert stale map. Cadence anchors that survive low engagement: AM's weekday briefing on trading days (existing), the daily next-step on all days (new), a Sunday-evening "your week's map builds tonight, here is one thing to study" touch, and a weekend "review your week" recap so off-session days have both a reason to open and a reason to come back.
