title: Open read routine - grade trend first
category: method
source: Anne-Marie Baiynd "Blueprint Open" daily videos (2026)

Before the market opens, establish the day's structural bias with a five-minute routine. This grade runs once and frames whether you hunt dips or sell bounces for the whole session.

**The process:** Pull the 30-minute chart. Read the alignment and slope of the 20, 50, and 200 SMAs. Positive trend (averages rising, stacked bullishly) says dips are buys. Negative trend (averages falling, bears in charge) says bounces are sell zones. Sideways (50 flat, 200 flat, pivots clustered) says trade the edges only, stand aside from the middle.

Next, layer structure: Fibonacci retracements peak-to-base and base-to-peak anchored to the prior dominant candle or opening bar, checking the 50% retest floor and extension targets. Read the overnight inventory (net-long says expect a dip-unwind; net-short says expect a bounce-fade). Mark the gamma walls (call walls above price as resistance, put walls below as support) and note if they are inverted or in sync. Anchored VWAP to the prior-day close or the 9:30 open, rising or flat or falling.

Watch the 4am London candle and the 200 SMA on the 4-hour: they are your macro confirmation that bias is aligned across timeframes.

That's it. Grade complete. Now you trade inside that grade until the trend gate flips.
