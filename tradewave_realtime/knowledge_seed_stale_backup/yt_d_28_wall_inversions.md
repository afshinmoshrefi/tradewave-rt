---
title: Confused option positioning and wall inversions
category: method
source: Anne-Marie Baiynd "Blueprint Open" daily videos (2026)
---
When the call wall sits below the put wall, or zero-DTE and month-end walls point in opposite directions, option traders are structurally offside and will be forced to re-hedge. This positioning tell reliably precedes outsized swings as one side gets flushed out. When the walls are in sync, expect consensus and a calmer range. When they are inverted, expect a squeeze. Read the walls against each other, not just against price.
