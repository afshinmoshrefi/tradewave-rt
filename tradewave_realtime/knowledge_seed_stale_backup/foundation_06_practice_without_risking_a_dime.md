Here is the best news in this whole course: you can learn to trade without putting one real dollar at risk. None. Zero.

It's called **paper trading**, or **sim** (short for simulator). "Paper" is an old phrase from when people practiced with pencil and paper. Today it's built right into the software. The platform shows you the **real, live market** with real prices and real candles, but you trade with **fake money**. Your wins and losses are pretend. The chart is not.

**Most trading platforms offer a free simulator.** You usually flip a switch labeled "Sim" or "Paper" or "Demo," and now you're in practice mode. Look for it before you ever fund a real account. If you can't find it, the coach in this app can point you to where it lives on common platforms.

Placing a practice trade works exactly like a real one. You pick buy or sell, you choose your order type (remember our limit orders), you set your size (one micro is plenty), and you submit. Then you watch what happens and you take notes on what you saw.

And that last part is the whole point. The goal of practice is **not** to rack up fake profits. The goal is to **practice the read first**, to train your eyes to see the setup before you ever risk a cent. The money will still be there after your eyes are ready.

So go slow. A practice trade you got wrong but understood is worth more than a lucky one you didn't. And if you sit on your hands all session and place nothing, that counts too. **A zero-trade day is a win.** The buffet is open every single day. You are not going to miss it.
