The 200 SMA is the primary long-term filter and a mean-reversion target, not just a binary gate. When price drifts far above the rising 20, 50, and 200, demand is exhausting and price tends to rotate back into the averages: that compression back toward them is the dip-buy setup, and the averages tightening is the signal it is near. Read the 200 across timeframes (30-minute, hourly, 4-hour, daily): in an uptrend, bounces into a rising 200 are buy zones, and a clean break below it on higher frames flips it to a short signal. The 50 is secondary structure, the 20 is the near-term momentum read. The 200 is the most statistically significant line on the chart.
