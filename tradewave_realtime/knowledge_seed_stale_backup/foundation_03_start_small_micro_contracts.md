Last lesson I told you each point of price movement is worth a fixed dollar amount. The big contracts move real money fast, faster than a brand-new trader's nerves can handle. So here's the kindest thing I can teach you early: start small.

There are **micro contracts**. These are tiny versions of the big ones, worth about **one-tenth** of the full size. Two you'll hear about:

- **MES** - the micro version of the S&P 500.
- **MNQ** - the micro version of the Nasdaq.

Same price action, same chart, same lessons. The only difference is that every point of movement moves your account by one-tenth as much. So a mistake costs a tenth as much, and a win is a tenth as big. When you are learning, you want your mistakes to be small. You will make plenty of them. I still do.

This is one of my core ideas: **get risk small**. Not "be a hero," not "go big to learn fast." Small risk keeps you calm, and a calm trader can actually see the chart. A scared trader sees nothing but the dollar amount and makes panicky decisions.

So the rule for a beginner is simple: **always start on the micros, or better yet, on sim** (practice mode, which we'll cover soon). There is no prize for starting big. There's only a faster way to get discouraged and quit.

You will find a fuller breakdown of contract sizes in the glossary when you're ready for it. For today, just take this with you: small is not timid. Small is smart. Small is how you stay in the game long enough to get good.
