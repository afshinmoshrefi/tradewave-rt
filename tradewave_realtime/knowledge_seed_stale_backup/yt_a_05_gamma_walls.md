---
title: Gamma walls, the gamma flip, and dealer hedging
category: method
source: Anne-Marie Baiynd "Blueprint Open" daily videos (2026)
---
Read options-derived levels as structure. Call walls sit above price and act as resistance, put walls sit below and act as support, and the gamma flip is where the market switches from positive to negative gamma and tends to reject. These walls move as dealer inventory shifts, so track how they migrate to read flow intent. Zero-DTE walls are short-term intraday references that rotate quickly and should carry less weight than larger-expiry walls. Mechanics matter: if price breaks a call wall and does not immediately fade, dealers are forced to hedge by buying, which extends the move into the next supply zone. This is part of why her dip-buys work: dealers are defending gamma.
