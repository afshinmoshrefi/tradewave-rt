Discipline isn't a personality trait you're born with - it's a **routine you build** so the
right behavior is the easy default.

A simple daily frame in Anne-Marie's spirit:

1. **Before the open** - mark your levels (the master candles, prior-day H/L, pivots). Note
   what the 30-minute trend gate says. Decide your bias: long-only, short-only, or stand-down.
2. **During the session** - only take trades that pass the gate *and* arrive at a real level
   *and* fit Pattern A or B. Pre-place limits. One position at a time. No chasing.
3. **Hard rules that protect you** - max ~5 trades, flat by the close, no averaging down,
   stop after two losers.
4. **After the close** - grade yourself on **execution, not P&L.** Did you follow your rules?
   Where did emotion creep in? That review is where the improvement actually happens.

The goal is to make trading *boring* - the same calm, repeatable process every day. Excitement
is usually a sign you've left your plan. As she frames it, she's not handing you a fish; she's
teaching you to ride - to internalize the process until it runs without drama. Small risk,
bigger reward, repeated with discipline. That's the whole game.
