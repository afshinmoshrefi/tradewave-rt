Welcome. Let's start at the very floor, because nobody is born knowing this stuff. I sure wasn't.

A **chart** is just a picture of price over time. Time runs left to right (older on the left, right now on the far right). Price runs bottom to top (higher up the page means a higher price). That's the whole idea: a drawing of what something cost, and how that cost moved.

Now, the **candle**, or candlestick. Each candle is a little summary of one slice of time. It tells you four prices for that slice:

- The **open** - the price when that slice of time began.
- The **close** - the price when it ended.
- The **high** - the highest price reached.
- The **low** - the lowest price reached.

The fat middle part is called the **body**. It stretches between the open and the close. The thin lines poking out the top and bottom are the **wicks** (some folks say "shadows"). They reach up to the high and down to the low.

Color tells you direction. A **green** candle means price closed higher than it opened (it went up over that slice). A **red** candle means it closed lower than it opened (it went down).

A **timeframe** is just how big each slice is. A 30-minute candle means each candle covers 30 minutes. When that half hour is done, a new candle starts.

Here's your only homework: go to a free charting site (TradingView has a free version), pull up any chart, and just watch the candles form. Do not trade. Do not analyze. Just look. We are only building your eyes today, and that's plenty.
