**Instruments.** Anne-Marie trades ES, NQ, CL (crude), GC (gold), and RTY. *"I do ES, NQ,
CL, RTY. I don't normally trade the YM."* For small or evaluation accounts she'll use the
micros (MES, MNQ).

**Timeframes.** The 30-minute chart decides direction and day-type. The 1-minute chart is
for the micro entries at levels. On cautious or sideways days she wants more confirmation
(a two-candle confirm) before committing.

**Time-of-day matters:**
- Globex opens 6:00 PM ET. The cash session opens 9:30 AM ET.
- The open is noisy - she watches the first 30 minutes rather than entering on the opening
  break. There are recognizable algo-push windows in the first hour.
- The 1:30 PM candle is a known afternoon turn/expansion marker.
- Cancel unfilled limits in the early afternoon and be **flat by the close**.

**Instrument quirks:** Camarilla pivots work on NQ but not ES. Crude's institutional candle
is 10:00 AM (not 3:30), and crude winds down earlier. Micro gold's book is thin - limits
only, no market orders.

You don't trade every instrument every day. You trade the one that's giving you a clean,
trend-aligned level at a time of day that makes sense.
