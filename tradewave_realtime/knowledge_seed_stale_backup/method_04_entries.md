---
title: Two entries - the limit and the failed retest
category: method
source: Anne-Marie method (distilled)
---
A level is only a candidate. Anne-Marie takes it two ways, and **always with limit orders - 
never market orders** (a market order in a thin book gets chewed up).

**Pattern A - the pre-placed limit.** On a clean trend day, you place your limit at the
trend-aligned level *in advance* and let price come to you. No chasing. Price does the work.

**Pattern B - the failed retest (her default).** *"I rarely take a breakdown trade. What I
will take is a failed retest - a look above and fail, a look below and fail."* Price pokes
through the level to run the obvious stops, then closes back on the original side. That
"breach candle" is the tell. You enter as price reclaims the level, with your stop just
beyond the breach candle's extreme.

The logic of both: you want price *rejecting* a level in your direction, confirmed by the
trend gate - not a hopeful guess that a level will hold. The failed retest is powerful
because it traps the crowd that chased the obvious break, and you're positioned the other
way as they cover.

Confirmation she layers on: VWAP on the right side, momentum agreeing, enough volume, and
the level not being an exhaustion zone (R3/R4 territory = fade, don't chase).
