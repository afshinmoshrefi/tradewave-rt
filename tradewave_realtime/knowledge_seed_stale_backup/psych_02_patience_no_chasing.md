---
title: Patience - the buffet is open tomorrow
category: psychology
source: Anne-Marie trading psychology (distilled)
---
The hardest skill in day trading isn't finding setups - it's **not taking the ones that
aren't there.** Anne-Marie's antidote to FOMO is a phrase: *"the buffet is open tomorrow."*

If you miss a level, you let it go. You do not chase price after it's already moved - that's
how you buy the top of a run right before it reverses. There is always another setup, another
session, another day. The market is not running out of opportunities.

Two mantras to internalize:
- *"Go to the second-prettiest girl."* (From prospect theory.) Don't hold out for the one
  perfect, gorgeous setup - take the **good-enough, statistically sound** setup that's
  actually in front of you, sized appropriately. Perfectionism is just FOMO in a nicer suit.
- *"A zero-trade day is a win."* If nothing lines up with your rules, doing nothing is the
  correct, professional outcome - not a wasted day.

When you feel the itch to chase, that's the signal to slow down, not speed up. The patient
trader lets price come to the level. The impatient trader brings their money to price. One
of those gets paid.
