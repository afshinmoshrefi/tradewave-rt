---
title: Crosscurrents, when the timeframes disagree
category: psychology
source: Anne-Marie Baiynd "Blueprint Open" daily videos (2026)
---
When timeframes disagree on trend, it is a battleground that demands tighter risk and smaller size, or standing aside. Trend is up, momentum is down is the canonical crosscurrent. Name the disagreement out loud instead of forcing a side. A crosscurrent is not a reason to trade harder, it is a reason to trade smaller or wait. The cleanest setups come when the timeframes line up.
