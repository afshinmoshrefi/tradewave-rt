The market isn't open all the time, and the parts that are open don't all behave the same way. Knowing the clock matters, so let's lay it out. All times are Eastern (ET), because that's where the exchanges keep time.

**Regular hours** run from **9:30 AM to 4:00 PM ET**, Monday through Friday. This is the busy, lively part of the day, when the most people are trading. We call 9:30 AM **the open** and 4:00 PM **the close**.

But futures also trade overnight, in a session often called **Globex**. It runs through the evening and into the early morning, before regular hours begin again. It's usually quieter and thinner, meaning fewer people are trading, which can make price jumpy and harder to read.

**Weekends and most holidays, the market is closed.** No candles form. Nothing moves. That's a feature, not a problem. It's rest.

Now, why does any of this matter to you? Two reasons. First, the **open** is often the most active, emotional stretch of the day, full of fast moves. Second, calm and clarity beat chaos when you're learning.

I trade **mostly the morning**, the first couple of hours after the open. That's where the cleanest action tends to be for the way I read the market. I am not glued to the screen all day, and you shouldn't be either. The screen will wear you down.

You don't have to trade every session, or even most of them. Pick a window, learn its rhythm, and let the rest go by. Remember: **the buffet is open tomorrow.** The market will be right here when you come back. There is no move you have to catch today.
