---
title: The if-and-then conditional entry rule
category: method
source: Anne-Marie Baiynd "Blueprint Open" daily videos (2026)
---
Never anticipate, confirm. A valid entry needs two parts: the breakout AND a successful pullback that holds the broken level. If both happen, then you trade. If the level cannot be recaptured, you stay out. The corollary: a failed breakdown that recaptures the level confirms a reversal up, while a bounce that cannot recapture confirms continuation down. Break and hold, then act. This is her most repeated risk-structuring rule after the core: it keeps you out of the fake-out and in the real move.
