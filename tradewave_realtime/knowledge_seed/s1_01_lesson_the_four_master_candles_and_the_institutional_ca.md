Anne-Marie reads the day through a few **master candles** - 30-minute reference candles
whose highs and lows become the structure the day trades around (all times ET):

- **The institutional candle** - the 3:30 PM close. This is the most important; it's where
  size positions for the overnight. (For crude oil, the institutional candle is 10:00 AM.)
- **6:00 PM Globex open** - the overnight session open.
- **4:00 AM Europe open** - its high, low, and close are all reference points.
- **9:30 AM RTH open** - the day's opening range.

Walk them in order: 3:30 → 6 PM → 4 AM → 9:30.

The **institutional candle gets "validated" by volume** (her MOC idea): when the 3:30
candle's volume is meaningfully heavier than the 3:00 candle's, smart money was active and
the level carries more weight - trade it with more confidence and size. When that volume
isn't there, you reduce size and widen your expectations.

The point isn't to memorize times - it's the mindset: **price respects places where
institutions did business.** Those candles mark the field you play on.

Before Anne-Marie places a single trade, she reads a map of the day. That map is built from four specific 30-minute candles she calls "master candles" - and a candle, for the total beginner, is just a bar on a price chart that shows where price opened, closed, went high, and went low during a time window.

Think of a city with four key intersections. Traffic jams or clears based on what happened at those intersections earlier. Price does the same thing around these four time stamps (all Eastern Time):

1. 3:30 PM - the "institutional candle." This is the big one. Large funds and professional traders - the "institutions" - use this moment to set up overnight positions. Whatever price range gets carved out here becomes a reference zone the next session tends to respect. (For crude oil traders, the equivalent moment is 10:00 AM.)

2. 6:00 PM - the Globex open, meaning the overnight futures session officially begins here.

3. 4:00 AM - Europe comes online. The high, low, and close of that candle all become reference points.

4. 9:30 AM - the regular U.S. session open, giving you the opening range for the day.

She walks them in order: 3:30, then 6 PM, then 4 AM, then 9:30.

One refinement: the 3:30 candle carries extra weight when its volume - the total number of contracts traded - is noticeably heavier than the 3:00 candle's volume. That heavier volume tells you the professionals were active, so that level is worth more respect - trade it with more confidence and larger size. Lighter volume means you reduce your size and widen your expectations.

The core idea is simple: price tends to respect the places where big institutions did business. These four candles mark where that business happened.
