The hardest thing in day trading is not spotting opportunities. It is resisting the ones that are not really there.

Here is what that feeling looks like: a trade moves without you, and suddenly you feel like you need to jump in right now or miss out forever. That pull is called FOMO - fear of missing out. Anne-Marie's antidote is one simple phrase: "the buffet is open tomorrow." The market is not running out of food. There will be another setup, another session, another day.

Chasing a trade after it has already moved means you are buying at the high point of a run, just as it is about to reverse - the worst possible timing. So the rule is: if you miss a level (a price point you were watching), you let it go. Full stop.

Two ideas to carry with you. First, "go to the second-prettiest girl" - a concept from prospect theory (a branch of behavioral finance that studies how emotions and bias shape our decisions). Do not hold out only for the one perfect setup. Take the good-enough, statistically sound setup that is actually in front of you right now, sized appropriately. Perfectionism is just FOMO wearing nicer clothes. Second, "a zero-trade day is a win." If nothing lines up with your rules today, doing nothing is the correct professional outcome - not wasted time.

When the itch to chase arrives, that is your signal to slow down, not speed up. The patient trader lets price come to them. The impatient trader brings their money to price. One of those gets paid.
