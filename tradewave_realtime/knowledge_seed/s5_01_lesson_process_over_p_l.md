Anne-Marie's whole edge is psychological discipline wrapped around a simple method. The
first shift she asks of you: **stop measuring yourself by the money, and start measuring
yourself by the process.**

You do not control whether a given trade wins. You control whether you:
- waited for the trend gate to give permission,
- let the level come to you instead of chasing,
- entered with a limit and a planned stop,
- sized small, and
- exited level-to-level instead of hoping.

Do those five things and the money takes care of itself over a sample of trades. Obsess
over the money on any single trade and you'll start breaking the rules that produce it - 
moving stops, adding to losers, revenge trading.

She comes at this from a background in mathematics and neuroscience, so she's blunt about
it: trading is mostly **managing your own brain under uncertainty.** The chart is the easy
part. A good coach doesn't pump you up - it keeps returning you to your process when your
emotions want to take the wheel. Grade your day on execution, not on the number.
<!--PLAIN-->
Here is the single biggest mindset shift Anne-Marie asks of you, and it sounds simple until your money is on the line: stop grading yourself by whether you made money today, and start grading yourself by whether you followed your steps.

Think of it like learning to bake bread. A beginner judges success by whether the loaf looked perfect. A baker grades themselves on whether they followed the recipe - measured the yeast, let the dough rest the right amount of time, kept the oven at the right temperature. Do the steps right, and good loaves show up over many attempts. Skip the steps chasing a "perfect loaf" today, and you get inconsistent results forever.

Trading works exactly the same way. Anne-Marie - who comes from a background in mathematics and neuroscience - is blunt: reading the chart is actually the easy part. Managing your own brain under uncertainty is the hard part.

You do not control whether any single trade wins. You do control whether you waited for your trend gate (the filter that tells you which direction has permission), let the price come to the level instead of chasing it, entered at a limit price with a planned stop-loss (a limit order locks in your entry price; a stop-loss exits the trade automatically if price moves against you by a set amount), kept size small, and exited level-to-level (meaning you take profit at the next key price level, then reassess, rather than hoping it runs forever) without hoping for more.

Do those five things consistently and the money takes care of itself across many trades. Obsess over the money on one trade and you start moving stops, adding to losing positions, or revenge-trading to "get it back" - all rule-breakers.

Process over P&L. Grade your day on execution, not the number.
