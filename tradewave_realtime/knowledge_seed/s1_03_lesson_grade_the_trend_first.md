Before looking for any entry, grade the trend. Read the alignment and slope of the 20, 50, and 200 SMAs (mainly on the 30-minute, dropping to the hourly or 4-hour when the 30-minute is too noisy). Positive trend (averages rising and stacked bullishly): dips are buys. Negative trend (averages falling): bounces are sell zones. Sideways (the 50 flat, pivots clustered close together): trade the edges, not the middle, and wait for a breakout. Pick whichever timeframe shows the formation most cleanly rather than defaulting to a fixed one. This grade sets your bias for the whole session before a single level is considered.

Before you look at a single trade setup, Anne-Marie wants you to answer one question first: which way is the market leaning right now?

She does that by reading three moving averages - lines on a chart that smooth out the day's noise and show the average price over 20, 50, and 200 recent candles (think of them as a short memory, a medium memory, and a long memory of price). She watches these mostly on a 30-minute chart, but if that chart looks jumbled she steps back to the hourly or 4-hour chart to get a cleaner picture. The right timeframe is whichever one shows the story most clearly - there is no single correct default.

Here is how she reads them. If all three averages are rising and stacked neatly one above the other, the trend is positive - she treats dips in price as potential buying moments. If they are falling, the trend is negative - she treats small bounces upward as potential selling zones. If the middle average (the 50) is flat and the pivots, the little peaks and valleys, are bunched close together, the market is going sideways. In that case she waits for a breakout and only trades near the outer edges, not the middle of the range.

This "grade" - bullish, bearish, or sideways - becomes her bias for the whole session, before she even glances at a specific entry level.

Everything is a shade of gray, so grade honestly and let the chart tell you what it is. Want Anne-Marie to walk you through grading a live chart? Ask the coach, or read her full lesson for the complete details.
