**Instruments.** Anne-Marie trades ES, NQ, CL (crude), GC (gold), and RTY. *"I do ES, NQ,
CL, RTY. I don't normally trade the YM."* For small or evaluation accounts she'll use the
micros (MES, MNQ).

**Timeframes.** The 30-minute chart decides direction and day-type. The 1-minute chart is
for the micro entries at levels. On cautious or sideways days she wants more confirmation
(a two-candle confirm) before committing.

**Time-of-day matters:**
- Globex opens 6:00 PM ET. The cash session opens 9:30 AM ET.
- The open is noisy - she watches the first 30 minutes rather than entering on the opening
  break. There are recognizable algo-push windows in the first hour.
- The 1:30 PM candle is a known afternoon turn/expansion marker.
- Cancel unfilled limits in the early afternoon and be **flat by the close**.

**Instrument quirks:** Camarilla pivots work on NQ but not ES. Crude's institutional candle
is 10:00 AM (not 3:30), and crude winds down earlier. Micro gold's book is thin - limits
only, no market orders.

You don't trade every instrument every day. You trade the one that's giving you a clean,
trend-aligned level at a time of day that makes sense.

**When she actually trades.** Primarily the regular US session (9:30 AM to the 3:00 PM flat-by rule). She reads the overnight every morning - the Globex open and Europe candles are part of the daily map - and she will occasionally take a trade pre-open or in the Globex session when a clean setup forms at a level, but that is the exception, not the routine. Cancel resting limits by 2:30 PM ET (2:00 for crude), flat by 3:00 PM ET (2:30 for crude), and the institutional candle at 3:30-4:00 closes the day's read.

Anne-Marie does not trade everything on the board. She trades a small, familiar list: ES and NQ (the two big U.S. stock-index futures), CL (crude oil futures), GC (gold futures), and RTY (the small-cap Russell index future). She does not normally trade the YM. If an account is small or in an evaluation phase, she uses the "micro" versions - MES, MNQ - which are simply smaller-sized contracts of the same markets.

Two charts, two jobs. The 30-minute chart is where she decides the direction for the day and what type of day it looks like. The 1-minute chart is where she times the actual entry at a specific price level. On cautious or sideways days she waits for a two-candle confirm - meaning she lets the second candle close in her direction before committing - rather than jumping in on the first signal.

Every morning she also reads the overnight session, called Globex (that is the nearly 24-hour futures market that runs before the regular U.S. stock market opens at 9:30 AM Eastern). She checks what happened during Europe's trading hours too, because those overnight moves are part of the day's map. When a very clean setup forms at a clear level before the open, she will sometimes take a Globex trade - but that is the exception, not the everyday routine.

When does she actually trade? Mainly the regular U.S. session, 9:30 AM onward. She watches - not trades - the opening break, because the first 30 minutes are noisy and often driven by automated programs. The 1:30 PM candle is a known spot where the afternoon can shift direction. She cancels any open orders that have not been filled by 2:30 PM Eastern (2:00 PM for crude oil), and she is completely flat - meaning out of all positions - by 3:00 PM (2:30 for crude). The 3:30-4:00 PM institutional candle closes the day's read even after she is done trading.

Each instrument also has its own personality. A tool called Camarilla pivots works well on NQ but not on ES, so she applies it selectively. Crude oil's most important institutional candle prints at 10:00 AM, not the usual times, and crude winds down earlier in the day. Micro gold is a thin market - meaning fewer buyers and sellers are active - so she uses limit orders only, never market orders (a market order fills immediately at whatever price is available; a limit order fills only at the price she sets or better).

The core idea: trade the one instrument that is giving you a clean, clear setup at a time of day that makes sense. Not every instrument, not every session, every day.
