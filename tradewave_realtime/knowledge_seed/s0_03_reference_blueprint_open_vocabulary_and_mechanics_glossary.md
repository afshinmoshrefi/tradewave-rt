**Core mechanics and structures:**

- **Call wall:** Options-derived resistance where call sellers sit above price and defend; dealers must buy if price breaks above it without fading, extending the move further.
- **Put wall:** Options-derived support where put sellers sit below price and defend; acts as a cushion to short cascades and often reverses bounces.
- **Gamma flip:** The zone where market switches from positive to negative gamma, causing price to reject and retest the level; a reliability tell for mean reversion.
- **Gamma line:** The structural threshold from dealer gamma mechanics; acts as a go/no-go gate for trend continuation vs consolidation.
- **Overnight inventory:** Net-long or net-short positioning from the overnight session; long inventory predicts morning dip-unwind, short inventory predicts morning bounce-fade.
- **Anchored VWAP:** Volume-weighted average price locked to a specific candle (prior close, session open, or dominant bar) rather than generic VWAP; sharper mean-reversion target and inventory read.
- **Point of control (POC):** The price level with the highest traded volume in the session; acts as a magnet and frequent bounce target.
- **Value area:** The range where roughly 70% of trading volume traded; price tends to rotate back into value area; loss of value area low signals deeper pullback or reversal.
- **Measured move:** The height or width of a prior candle or pattern, projected from a breakout point to set target zones; anchored to dominant candles or flags.
- **Dominant candle:** The highest-volume or most significant candle on the chart (often the 9:30 RTH open or prior day's high-volume bar); sets the primary institutional flow direction for the session.
- **Backwardation / Contango:** Futures curve structure - backwardation lifts prices via roll cost and near-month premium; contango pressures via deferred-contract discount; mechanical driver independent of spot price, not directional bias.
- **Max pain:** Options expiration magnet; the strike price where the most options expire worthless; in gamma-pinned markets, price gravitates toward it, especially on expiration Fridays.
- **Reflexive bounce:** Mechanical, automatic bounce after heavy selling in a hedged market, not a reversal signal; use structure (look-above-and-fail vs look-below-and-fail) to confirm next direction.
- **Wall inversion:** Call wall sits below put wall (or zero-DTE and month-end walls point opposite directions); signals option traders are structurally offside and will be forced to re-hedge, preceding large swings.

**Entry and setup mechanics:**

- **If-and-then:** The conditional entry rule: if price breaks a level AND the broken level holds on pullback (recapture), then the move is real; if not, stay flat.
- **Recapture:** Price returns to and holds at a previously broken level; confirms move is real rather than a fake shake of the trees.
- **Confluence:** Two or more structural levels (pivot, VWAP, 50 SMA, Fibonacci) aligned at the same price; confluence raises conviction and entry quality.
- **Vertical stack / Landlocked:** Moving averages (200, 50, 20) and VWAP cluster tightly at the same price level; signals compression and big move imminent in the trend direction.
- **Fade / Pullback:** Price rotation back toward support or moving average after an initial move; the zone where entry risk improves and wait-for-the-fade rule applies.
- **Shake the trees:** Deliberate liquidity sweep past an obvious low or high to flush weak hands before the real move; a failed shake that recaptures confirms reversal direction.
- **Look above and fail / Look below and fail:** Break of a resistance that quickly reverses (bullish tell) or break of support that quickly reverses (bearish tell); exact opposite shows next direction.
- **Second prettiest girl:** The second test of a level after a failed first attempt; often better entry than initial breakout due to reduced leverage and clearer confirmation.

**Risk and process language:**

- **Dips are buys / Bounces are sells:** Directional stance given the open read: in uptrend, buy dips to structure, not the spikes; in downtrend, sell bounces, not the breaks.
- **Wait for the fade, never buy the breakout:** Core risk discipline: do not chase extended price; enter on pullback instead.
- **Everything is a shade of gray:** Markets rarely offer black-or-white setups; accept conflicting signals and shrink size rather than forcing clarity.
- **Crosscurrent:** Multi-timeframe disagreement (e.g., 30-min up, 4-hour neutral); signals battleground requiring tighter risk and smaller size or standing aside.
- **Process over P&L:** Track process adherence (wait for fade, check confluence, size for bias) over trade outcome; process compounds over time, luck does not.
- **Get risk small / Trade small or not at all:** Shrink size when bias is unclear, inventory is heavy, or volatility is elevated; a zero-trade day or micro day that respects process is a win.
- **Line in the sand:** Threshold between trend and chop; once 200 SMA breaks or SMA slopes flip, move the line; do not trade against the broken line.
- **Shake out the weak hands:** Stops are run and walls are tested; recognize the shake and do not panic into it; let structure confirm which way the real move intends.
- **Wall of worry:** Market that climbs over concerns and resistance; often precedes largest continuation moves; do not fight the wall of worry climb.
