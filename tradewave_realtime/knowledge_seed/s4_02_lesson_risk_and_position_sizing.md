The strategy is conservative by design - the edge is in the discipline, not in size.

- **Small size.** Around 2–3 ES contracts at most; micros for small or evaluation
  accounts. Counter-trend or unvalidated days → size down.
- **Max ~5 trades a day.** *"Usually my max max is five."* Some days you take zero. That's
  correct, not a failure.
- **No averaging down.** A broken level means the thesis was wrong - you're out, you don't
  add to a loser.
- **Stop after two consecutive losers.** Walk away; the market will be there tomorrow.
- **Keep risk small and let reward be bigger.** You don't need to be right often if your
  winners are bigger than your losers and your losers are capped.
- **Be flat by the close** (around 3:00 PM ET for index futures; earlier for crude). You
  cancel unfilled limits before then and don't carry intraday risk into the bell.

The number that matters most isn't your win rate - it's whether you **protected your
capital and followed your rules.** A day where you lost a little but traded your plan
perfectly is a good day. A day where you made money breaking every rule is a problem.

Think of this lesson as the "how much to put on the table" conversation - and the answer is: less than you think.

Anne-Marie keeps her trade size small on purpose. She works with around 2 to 3 ES contracts at a time - an "ES contract" is simply one unit of a futures trade on the S&P 500 index. If you have a smaller or practice account, she recommends micros, which are a fraction of the size of a standard contract and cost much less per move. On days when the market is going against the trend or when her read is not confirmed yet, she sizes down even further.

She also caps herself at about five trades a day. Some days she takes zero. That is not failure - that is the strategy working.

Two rules protect her capital most aggressively. First, no averaging down - meaning if a trade breaks the level she was watching, the idea was wrong and she gets out, period. She does not buy more of something that is already losing. Second, she stops trading entirely after two losses in a row. Walk away - the market will be there tomorrow.

The goal is to keep losses small and let the winning trades be bigger than the losing ones. You do not need to win most of your trades to come out ahead - you just need your winners to outrun your capped losers.

She is always flat - meaning out of all positions - by around 3:00 PM ET for index futures, and even earlier for crude oil. Any orders still waiting to fill get cancelled before then. No intraday trades are carried past the closing bell.

The number that matters most is not how often you won. It is whether you protected your capital and followed your plan. "Process over P and L." A day where you lost a little but traded your rules perfectly is a good day. A day where you made money by breaking every rule is a problem.
