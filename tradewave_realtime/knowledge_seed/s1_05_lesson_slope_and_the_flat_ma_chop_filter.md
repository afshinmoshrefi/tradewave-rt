Slope and the flat-MA chop filter - in plain English

Imagine you are driving and you want to know if the road ahead is going uphill, downhill, or flat. That angle tells you a lot - uphill takes more effort, flat means you might just coast, and the character of your drive changes completely.

Anne-Marie uses something similar called a moving average (MA), which is just the market's average price over a recent period. She watches three common ones: the 50-period, the 200-period, and the VWAP (which stands for Volume Weighted Average Price - think of it as the "fair price" most traders agreed on throughout the day, weighted by how much they traded at each level).

The key insight here is this: it is not enough to know whether price is above or below one of these averages. You also have to look at the angle, the slope, of the line itself.

If the moving average is climbing upward and price is sitting above it, the market is showing genuine momentum in one direction - that is when she considers a trend trade.

But if that average has gone flat or is starting to roll over - just running sideways or curling back - that is the market telling you it is undecided, stuck in chop (choppy, back-and-forth action with no clear winner). Trying to trade a trend through a flat average is like trying to run fast on a moving sidewalk that has stopped - it just grinds you.

There is also a specific situation to watch: if the VWAP goes flat while price is still technically in a trend, that is a warning sign. It usually means consolidation is setting in and a pullback or retest of that level is coming before the trend can continue.

Her rule is simple: flat or rolling equals stand aside. Wait for the compression to resolve before committing. Slope tells you the character of the next move before it happens.
