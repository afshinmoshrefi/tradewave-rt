The 1:30 PM ET candle is a known afternoon turn and expansion marker. Watch it for a shift in the afternoon trend; it often marks where the morning move pauses or reverses.
<!--PLAIN-->
Here is a simple truth that took me years to appreciate: certain times of day carry more weight than others. The market is not a random blur of noise from open to close - it breathes in rhythms, and experienced traders watch those rhythms the way a sailor watches tides.

The 1:30 PM Eastern time candle is one of those rhythmic moments. Think of the trading day like a highway. Morning rush hour (the open) is loud and fast. Then things settle into a midday lull. The 1:30 PM candle is like that second wave of traffic in the late afternoon - it signals that a new direction or a burst of energy may be starting.

What Anne-Marie is watching for here is a shift - the moment where the morning move (whether up or down) either runs out of steam and reverses, or picks up fresh fuel and continues. She calls it an "afternoon turn and expansion marker." A marker is simply a signpost: it does not guarantee anything, but it tells you to pay close attention right at that moment on the clock.

Practically, this means glancing at your chart right around 1:30 PM ET and asking: is the candle that just printed agreeing with my trade, or is it warning me that the morning trend is fading?

Process over profit and loss (P and L). You are not reacting - you are observing a known, recurring rhythm.
