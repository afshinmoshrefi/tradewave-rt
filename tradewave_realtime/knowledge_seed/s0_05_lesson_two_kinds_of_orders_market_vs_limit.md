An **order** is simply your instruction to the platform: "I want to buy" or "I want to sell." There are a few kinds, but you only need two to start. Let's keep it that simple.

A **market order** says: "Get me in (or out) right now, at whatever the going price is." It's fast, and it almost always fills. "Fill" just means your order actually happens, you got your shares or contracts. The catch is that "whatever the going price is" can be worse than the number you saw a half-second ago. Price moves. A market order chases it. So you might ask to buy at 5000 and actually get filled at 5001 or 5002. That gap is called **slippage**, and it adds up.

A **limit order** says: "Only fill me at this exact price or better, not worse." If price never reaches your number, the order just sits there and never happens. You give up the guarantee of getting filled, but in exchange you control the price you pay. No nasty surprises.

Here's how I work, and why: I use **limit orders, almost only limit orders**. I decide ahead of time the price I'm willing to pay, I place my limit there, and then I wait. I do not chase. I make **price come to me.** If it doesn't come, fine, I didn't want it at a worse number anyway.

That's a mindset as much as a mechanic. A market order is the tool of someone in a hurry. A limit order is the tool of someone who has a plan and the patience to wait for it. As a beginner, lean hard toward patience. The chart does not reward people who grab.
