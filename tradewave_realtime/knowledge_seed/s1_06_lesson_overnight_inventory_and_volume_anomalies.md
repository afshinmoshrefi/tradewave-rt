Read overnight and pre-market inventory: net-long overnight inventory makes a morning dip or unwind more likely, net-short makes a morning bounce more likely. A sudden, unusual burst of pre-market volume (for example, a 4 a.m. spike) signals news and stress are coming: be ready for a large move and size carefully.

<!--PLAIN-->
Before the stock market's official open - called the Regular Trading Hours session, or RTH - there is an overnight period where traders around the world are already active. Anne-Marie uses this quiet window to scout, the same way a hunter reads tracks before stepping into the woods. She is not taking positions yet. She is just gathering clues.

The clue she looks for first is "overnight inventory." Think of inventory like a store's back room. If most traders went home holding a lot of long positions - meaning they are betting prices will rise - there is a lean toward a dip or pullback in the morning, because those traders may want to sell once the main session opens. If they went home holding mostly short positions - betting prices will fall - there is a lean toward a bounce. It is a lean, not a rule. The market does not owe anyone a textbook move.

The second clue is a sudden, unusual spike in pre-market volume (how many shares or contracts change hands). A big 4 a.m. volume burst, for example, is the market whispering "news and stress are coming." That is your cue to expect a larger-than-normal move and be very careful about size.

Anne-Marie also looks at the 4-hour chart - a chart where each candle represents four hours of trading - to frame three things before the open: the dominant candle (which four-hour bar has the most weight and tells the clearest story about direction), volume accumulation (where contracts or shares have been quietly building up, suggesting strong interest at a price zone), and overnight intent (what the collective behavior of overnight traders implies about where the day may begin to pull). Together those three give her a fuller picture than price alone.

This whole routine is about showing up informed, not about carrying overnight risk into the day. Process over P and L.
