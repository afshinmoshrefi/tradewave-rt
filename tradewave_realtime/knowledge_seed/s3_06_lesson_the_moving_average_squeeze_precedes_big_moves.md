<!--PLAIN-->
Let's talk about two tools Anne-Marie uses constantly: the 50 SMA and the 200 SMA. SMA stands for "simple moving average" - it is the average price of an asset over the last 50 or 200 days. Think of it like a ship's wake: it smooths out the chop and shows you the general direction price has been traveling.

Here is the key idea. When those two averages start drifting toward each other - especially when one is angled upward and the other is angled downward - that is called convergence, or a squeeze. Price starts bouncing back and forth inside that tightening range, like a spring being compressed.

Here is what makes this so important: the squeeze tells you a big move is coming, but it does NOT tell you which way. That is the whole lesson. Do not guess the direction. Waiting for the answer is what separates disciplined traders from the ones who keep getting hurt.

So what do you do? Mark the top and the bottom of that tight range. Then wait. When price finally breaks free, that break confirms the direction. You trade the resolution, not the setup.

Anne-Marie treats a converging wedge pattern exactly the same way - pressure building toward a release. The shape does not matter as much as the principle: let the market tip its hand first. Do not pick a side early.
