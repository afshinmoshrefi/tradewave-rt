STAGE 5 - The Trader's Mind: Discipline, Routine, and Grading Your Day

Here is something nobody tells you at the start: discipline is not a personality type you are born with. It is a routine you build so the right behavior becomes the easy, automatic default.

Anne-Marie breaks the trading day into a simple repeating frame.

Before the open, you mark your key price levels - the "master candles" (the highest-significance price bars from prior sessions), the prior day's high and low, and your pivot points (calculated reference prices the market tends to notice). You check what your trend gate says - the 30-minute gate is a filter that tells you whether to look for buys, sells, or nothing at all. You pick a bias and commit: long-only (looking to buy dips), short-only (looking to sell bounces), or stand-down (sit on your hands).

During the session, a trade only gets your attention if it passes the gate AND arrives at a real level AND fits Pattern A or B. Pre-place your limit orders rather than chasing a price that has already moved away from you. One position at a time.

Four hard rules protect you every single day: no more than roughly five trades, flat by the close (no overnight surprises), no averaging down (never adding to a losing position to try to "fix" it), and stop trading after two consecutive losses.

After the close, grade yourself on execution - NOT on profit and loss. Did you follow your rules? Where did emotion sneak in? That honest review is where the real improvement happens.

The goal is to make trading boring - the same calm, repeatable process every session. Excitement is usually a sign you have left your plan. As Anne-Marie puts it, she is not handing you a fish; she is teaching you to ride - to internalize the process until it runs without drama. Small risk, bigger reward, repeated with discipline. That is the whole game.
