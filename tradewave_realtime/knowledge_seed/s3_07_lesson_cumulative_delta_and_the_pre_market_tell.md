Cumulative delta flipping negative pre-market reveals seller dominance before the open, which tells you whether to fade or buy the first move. Read delta as a confirmation layer on top of overnight inventory and the wall map. Rising cumulative delta with rising price is real buying, while price up on flat or falling delta warns the move is hollow and likely to reverse. Use delta to confirm, not to lead.
<!--PLAIN-->
Before the opening bell, the market is already talking - you just need to know how to listen.

Here is the idea. Every trade that happens is either a buyer agreeing to a seller's price, or a seller agreeing to a buyer's price. "Cumulative delta" is simply a running score: add up all the trades where buyers were the aggressor (they paid the higher asking price), then subtract all the trades where sellers were the aggressor (they accepted the lower bid price). Think of it like a tug-of-war scoreboard that updates tick by tick.

When that scoreboard is running negative before the market opens, it tells you sellers are in control of the rope, even before most people show up. Anne-Marie calls that a "pre-market tell." It is one of the first clues that shapes whether you should look to sell a pop (the "fade") or buy a dip when the session begins.

Now here is the critical nuance, and it is worth writing on a sticky note. Delta is a confirmation layer, not a leader. You read it after you have already checked your overnight inventory (where price spent the night) and your "wall map" (the option-flow levels where big players tend to defend or attack). Delta confirms what those tools already suggested.

There are two signals to watch. First, the clean read: when price is rising and delta is also rising, real buyers are behind the move. That is the market telling you the strength is genuine. Second, the warning: when price is rising but delta is flat or falling, the move is hollow. Buyers are not really behind it. That mismatch is a warning, not an invitation.

Process over P and L - confirm before you commit.
